# Journals

An overview of academic journals relevant for industrial ecology research.

> *Generated with [TianGong Chat](https://chat.tiangong.world/) on 7 November 2023*

- **Journal of Industrial Ecology**: This journal promotes the concept of an industrial system that operates in harmony with surrounding ecosystems, aiming to optimize material flows and cycles. It covers topics such as industrial symbiosis, life cycle assessment, and sustainable infrastructure. Url: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/15309290

- **International Journal of Life Cycle Assessment**: This journal emphasizes diverse approaches to industrial ecology, including life cycle assessment, metabolism, and symbiosis. It is dedicated to promoting change to alleviate negative environmental conditions. Url: https://link.springer.com/journal/11367

- **Journal of Cleaner Production**: This journal focuses on the application of industrial ecology to address environmental concerns and promote sustainability. It covers a wide range of topics, including cleaner production, sustainable development, and environmental management. Url: https://www.journals.elsevier.com/journal-of-cleaner-production

- **Economic Systems Research**: This journal explores the integration of human systems with natural systems, minimizing energy and material use as well as minimizing the ecological impacts of human activity. It covers topics such as sustainable infrastructure and sustainable urban systems. Url: https://www.tandfonline.com/toc/cesr20/current

- **Ecological Economics**: This journal focuses on the relationships between human actions and the natural world, reflecting limits in the current reliance on command-and-control regulation in much of the industrialized world. It covers topics such as ecological economics and sustainable development. Url: https://www.journals.elsevier.com/ecological-economics

- **Nature Sustainability**: This journal focuses on the promotion of environmentally sound manufacturing and consumption. It aims to balance industrial development with the sustainable use of natural resources, including energy, materials, and the capacity of the environment to assimilate wastes and render valuable services. Url: https://www.nature.com/natsustain/
